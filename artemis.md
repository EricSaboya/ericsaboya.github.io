## Atmospheric Regional Trace gas Emissions Modeling Inverse System


<div id="essay1" style="margin-bottom: 60px;">


In Greek mythology, Artemis is the goddess of hunting and is considered a fierce protector of nature. My role as an atmospheric scientist is to determine greenhouse gas emissions and ensure substances banned under international agreements are not being released into the atmosphere. 

In the atmospheric sciences, inverse models are often used for inferring surface emissions from atmospheric trace gas measurements. ARTEMIS is a Bayesian inverse system which I co-developed for use in the MIT atmospheric inverse modeling group. Unlike other regional inverse models, ARTEMIS uses an ensemble of approaches for producing robust regional emission estimates. We have built in three different inverse methods and two different basis function algorithms tailored towards inferring both point and area source emissions. ARTEMIS has been optimized for fast performance and is an open-source project. Further information can be found <a href="https://github.com/mit-inverse-modelling/mit_inversions">here</a>.
